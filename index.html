Muhammed Ali

The greatest of all time

Timeline of Muhammed Ali :

  • 1942 - Cassius Clay is born in Louisville, Kentucky
  • 1960 - Clay was just 18 when he won the gold medal at Rome 1960 Olympic Games
  • 1964 - At 22, Cassius Clay defeats champion Sonny Liston in a technical knockout to win the world heavyweight boxing crown and change his name to Muhammed Ali one month Later.
  • 1971 - “The Fight of the Century” takes place between Ali and Joe Frazier in New York’s Madison Square Garden. Frazier won by a unanimous decision in 15 rounds. The bout marked Ali’s first defeat of his pro career.
  • 1974 - Ali and Frazier have a rematch in Madison Square Garden. This time, Ali won in 12 rounds.
  • 1975 - Ali took on Frazier for a third (and final) time, this time in the Philippines. Ali beat Frazier in a TKO in the 14th round in what is forever known as “The Thrilla in Manila”.
  • 1978 - Ali loses to 1976 Olympic champion Leon Spinks in 15 rounds by a split decision.
  • 1979 - Ali announces his retirement.
  • 2016 - Ali dies at age 74 in Phoenix, after being hospitalized with respiratory issues.
